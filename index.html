Basic Information about space.

A Vacuum.

Outer space is a zone that occurs about 100 kilometers (60 miles) above the planet, where there is no appreciable air to breathe or to scatter light

Can't hear a thing!

Since space is a vacuum, sound cannot be carried because molecules are not close enough together to transmit sound between them.

Speedy yet slow

Objects in space orbit at a fast pace, for instance, satellites can reach a speed of thousands of miles per hour!

Pluto, a planet or not?

A planet is a celestial body that orbits the sun, is massive enough to have a nearly round shape, and has cleared its orbit of debris. Therefore, the IAU has concluded Pluto to be a dwarf planet.

Space Vacation?

Chris Hadfield, a famous astronaut, went to space twice, and stayed for a long time there. Fortunately, he filmed his stay in the ISS!

Everyone loves Space

Not only does the USA (NASA) operate for space purposes, we have Russia(Roscosmos), Japan(JAXA), Canada(CSA) and many more conutries with space centres dedicated for space!

Habitable Satellites?

Did you know that, not only did we send normal satellites, but we sent habitable satellites too! Currently there're are two habitable satellites. The ISS (International Space Station) and the Tiangong-2. As for normal satellites, there are a plenty of sats ejected into Earth's orbit and further beyond.

Mercury

The fast, and the small

It is named after the Roman messenger god Mercury, the fastest Roman god. The planet Mercury was known by ancient people thousands of years ago.

Venus

Earth's twin

Venus is the only planet to be named after a female god, the goddess that symbolized beauty and love because it was the brightest planet known to ancient astronomers.

Earth

Our Planet

The name "Earth" is derived from both English and German words, 'eor (th)e/ertha' and 'erde', respectively, which mean ground. But, the handle's creator is unknown. One interesting fact about its name: Earth is the only planet that wasn't named after a Greek or Roman god or goddess.

Mars

The red planet

Mars is named after the Roman god of war. Many believe that ancient peoples associated Mars with bloodshed and war because of its red color.

Jupiter

The Gas Giant

The Romans named the planet after their king of gods, Jupiter, who was also the god of the sky and of thunder. Why choose to name the planet Jupiter? It was the largest object in the sky; therefore the most powerful; therefore Jupiter.

Saturn

The beauty of our solar-system

Like all of the planets, Saturn is named after a character in Roman mythology. Saturn is named after the god Saturnus, the god of agriculture and harvest.

Uranus

The Sided planet

The name "Uranus" is a reference to the Greek god of the sky, Uranus. According to Greek mythology, Uranus was the grandfather of Zeus (Jupiter) and father of Cronus (Saturn).

Neptune

The Blue

Later, when telescopes were used, other planets were discovered. Astronomers decided to continue naming the planets after Roman Gods. Neptune, a blueish planet, was named after the Roman god of the sea.
