Saturn, the most beautiful planet

NASA's Cassini spacecraft has been snapping amazing photos of Saturn and its moons from 2004 to 2017.

[1st picture: Feb. 26, 2016] The spacecraft captured this image from roughly 1.7 million miles, at 16 degrees above the ring plane using its wide-angle camera. The image reveals the planet’s odd hexagonal cloud pattern around the north pole.
[2nd picture: Aug. 22, 2009] It is not shown clearly, but Saturn’s rings are made of ice, rock & dust. They're still a beauty to look at.
[3rd picture: Feb. 2016]The planet’s odd hexagonal cloud pattern (around the north pole) can be seen as well.
[4th picture: July 14, 2014]The Saturnian moons of Titan, Hyperion and Prometheus are visible just beyond the planet's trademark rings.
[5th picture: January 2015] Saturn's dark side looms large in this image. Barely visible in the bottom-left corner is Tethys, one of Saturn's moons. The rings of Saturn are a dazzling accessory in this image from Cassini.
